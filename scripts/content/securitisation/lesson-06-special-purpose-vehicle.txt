A caterer collects advance payments for one hundred weddings. Those deposits are valuable, but leaving them in the caterer's everyday bank account exposes them to rent, payroll, suppliers, and every creditor of the business. A separate account helps, yet an account alone does not own contracts, issue securities, or survive the company's insolvency.

Securitisation needs a legal holder with a deliberately narrow job. The special purpose vehicle, spoken as S P V, acquires defined assets or rights, issues or supports securities, holds transaction accounts, and distributes cash under the documents. Its purpose is separation and execution, not business expansion.

Picture one vehicle-loan receivable moving from a lender to the S P V. The lender receives consideration. The vehicle becomes entitled to the transferred right. Investors fund securities linked to the pool. The servicer may continue interacting with the borrower, but the collection is meant to travel to accounts controlled for the transaction.

The S P V is often called bankruptcy remote. That phrase describes an objective, not an automatic property. A company name, trust deed, or registration certificate cannot by itself prevent creditors from challenging the transfer or courts from examining how the vehicle was actually operated.

Why not let the originator issue the notes directly? It can, but then investors may hold a claim exposed to the originator's entire balance sheet and insolvency. A separate vehicle is intended to make the identified pool, rather than every business asset and liability, the primary source of payment.

Limited purpose is the first discipline. The vehicle should not open factories, hire a sales force, speculate in unrelated assets, or incur ordinary commercial debts. Every extra activity creates another route through which losses, creditors, or management decisions could reach the transaction.

Separateness is the daily behaviour supporting the legal distinction. The S P V maintains its own records, accounts, governance, contracts, and decision process. It does not casually pay the originator's bills, lend its cash back without authority, or present itself as merely another department of the sponsor.

Independent governance can matter when the sponsor wants the vehicle to take an action that benefits the sponsor but harms investors. An independent director, trustee, or equivalent decision-maker may be required for specified insolvency or amendment decisions. Independence reduces a conflict; it does not replace clear contractual powers or competent administration.

Limited recourse means investors agree that repayment is limited to specified transaction assets and support. If collateral cash is insufficient after the documents are applied, investors generally cannot pursue the S P V as though it owned an unlimited operating business. The claim is intentionally bounded by the financed pool and agreed protections.

Non-petition provisions commonly restrict transaction parties from initiating insolvency proceedings against the vehicle until stated conditions are met. They reduce the chance that one disappointed creditor destroys the payment arrangement for everyone. Their effectiveness depends on applicable law and the parties bound by them.

Now follow the cash account. Borrower payments arrive, the servicer reconciles them, and the trustee or account bank applies control rules. If the S P V legally owns the receivables but the originator can freely withdraw collections, the economic separation is incomplete. Ownership and cash control must point in the same direction.

The vehicle also issues the securities. Note terms specify interest, principal, maturity, priority, events of default, voting, and remedies. The S P V does not decide each month whom it feels like paying. The waterfall and transaction documents make that decision in advance.

Suppose the originator becomes insolvent. Investors need the transferred assets to remain available to the S P V and collections to continue reaching controlled accounts. A true-sale opinion may support the legal conclusion. Servicing replacement, data access, powers of attorney, account control, and backup arrangements support the operational outcome.

Now suppose borrowers default while the originator remains perfectly healthy. The S P V has performed its separation function, yet the pool still loses cash. Legal isolation protects assets from the wrong creditors. It does not protect investors from the borrowers whose obligations form the assets.

This distinction is easy to miss because the same senior note can benefit from both legal isolation and credit enhancement. The first protects the claim from sponsor insolvency risk. The second absorbs some collateral loss. One cannot substitute for the other.

An S P V can take different legal forms across jurisdictions: a company, trust, partnership, or another permitted vehicle. The form affects governance, ownership, taxation, and enforcement. The analytical question remains constant: does the entity have the rights, restrictions, and controls required to perform its narrow function?

The Reserve Bank of India's securitisation framework and transfer directions establish conditions relevant to Indian transactions. United States asset-backed securities disclosures identify transaction parties, asset transfers, servicing, and payment mechanics for investors. These sources show what must be documented, but a public rulebook cannot prove that a particular vehicle observed separateness every day.

Diligence therefore goes beyond reading the vehicle's name. Review constitutional documents, the sale agreement, trust or security documents, account-control terms, limited-purpose covenants, permitted liabilities, governance, tax treatment, legal opinions, servicing transition rights, and amendment thresholds.

Imagine the S P V receives one crore rupees of collections. Fees are due, a reserve must be replenished, senior interest is payable, and principal follows. The vehicle is the legal and contractual junction through which those claims are applied. It is not the economic source of the money; borrowers are.

Music royalties expose the same limit. An artist transfers defined royalty rights into a dedicated vehicle that issues securities. The vehicle cannot make future songs successful. It can only hold the transferred rights, control the received royalties, and distribute them under the agreed priority.

That boundary captures the S P V precisely. It is not a magic box that improves assets. It is a narrow legal bridge designed to keep ownership, cash control, and payment rules standing when the company on the other side is under pressure.
