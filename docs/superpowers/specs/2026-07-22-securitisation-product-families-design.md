# Securitisation Product Families Audio Curriculum Design

## Goal

Expand the securitisation audio curriculum from its current mechanics-first 25-lesson set into a complete product-family curriculum without weakening the existing case-led, human, evidence-grounded teaching style.

## Design decision

Keep the existing seven documentary episodes and 25 lessons intact. Add a second product-family arc with distinct lessons for ABS, MBS, RMBS, and CMBS, followed by consumer and receivables ABS, CLOs, CDOs, esoteric/future-flow structures, and adjacent market infrastructure.

The three core labels are deliberately separate:

- ABS introduces the umbrella, collateral taxonomy, legal cash-flow rights, Regulation AB, pool data, and the distinction between a product label and a risk conclusion.
- MBS explains the mortgage-backed family and the agency/non-agency distinction.
- RMBS and CMBS then teach the different collateral engines: household mortgage behaviour versus commercial property cash flow, valuation, leasing, refinancing, and special servicing.

## Teaching design

Every new lesson must begin with a concrete payment, property, borrower, asset, or funding problem. The speakers invite a prediction before naming the structure. The script then follows the legal claim, cash path, timing, servicing or management decision, failure condition, protection, and investor consequence.

Each lesson must contain:

1. A human-scale opening mechanism.
2. A named real transaction, market event, regulatory document, or primary-source evidence spine.
3. A formal definition after the mechanism is visible.
4. Concrete cash-flow movement with units and dates.
5. A failure tension and the structural response.
6. A distinction from nearby products.
7. A transfer case, including an Indian bridge where the concept genuinely applies.
8. An interview-useful decision question and a memorable closing principle.

The case supports the concept and never replaces the full concept. Product labels cannot substitute for collateral analysis, legal rights, servicing, waterfall, protection, timing, or price.

## Voice and prose rules

- Diya remains calm, reflective, and precise.
- Meera remains skeptical, interruptive, and technically corrective.
- Interruptions occur at natural clause or thought boundaries.
- Spoken paragraphs remain continuous thoughts; Azure chunking is transport-only.
- Scripts use long, substantive dialogue with real disagreement and finance-specific judgement.
- Avoid robotic headings, classroom labels, stock interview prompts, generic case bridges, "this lesson," tutorial language, and architecture-diagram vocabulary.
- Avoid raw number dumps; every number receives a unit, date, and analytical purpose.
- Use clear spoken forms or deterministic pronunciation controls for ABS, RMBS, CMBS, MBS, CLO, CDO, ABCP, DSCR, LTV, PSA, SPV, and named transactions.
- Do not create forced pauses, giggles, stage directions, or sentence-fragment SSML paragraphs.

## Evidence and publication rules

Each new topic receives a source map before drafting. Primary evidence should come from SEC/Regulation AB, Federal Reserve or Treasury material, BIS/Basel material, RBI directions and reports, SIFMA where the convention is authoritative, deal documents, investor reports, rating-pool reports, and company or trustee filings.

Lessons with incomplete source captures remain drafts and must expose the missing evidence rather than inventing figures. A case may provide context, but the mechanism must remain generalisable beyond that case.

## Coverage contract

The final audit must prove that every new catalog topic has:

- one canonical ID and one source file;
- one episode assignment and stable sequence;
- a definition, mechanism, risk, failure, protection, comparison, and transfer case;
- at least one primary evidence anchor;
- pronunciation coverage for all acronyms and transaction names;
- generated audio text, SSML, MP3, and manifest;
- no retired speaker cues, bracketed narration markers, canned transitions, or abrupt paragraph boundaries.

## Verification

Run the securitisation catalog, content-depth, documentary-discovery, audio-script, Azure narration-surface, pronunciation, lint, and production-build checks. Generate SSML before MP3, inspect the new pronunciation and paragraph boundaries, then regenerate MP3s from the verified script package. Do not claim the listening experience changed until the refreshed MP3s exist and are spot-checked.

## Scope boundary

The product-family arc is broad enough to cover the repository's current securitisation inventory. Further specialist topics can be added later only when they have a distinct mechanism and evidence base; the curriculum should not become a glossary of labels without cash-flow teaching.
